Excerpts of Walden

Here, here is life
an experiment
to a great extent
untried by me.

I, shouldn’t talk so much,
oh about myself,
if there were anyone else
I knew as well.

You can’t kill time without
hurting eternity, no how,
oh and I know how.
I can’t improve the nick of time,
notch it on my stick
and stand out of line,
this time, it’s mine.

Pain, well I promise to pay
a quick visit tomorrow
before I die, today.

So I’ll survey
this world through scopes and shapes,
never natural eyes;
we’re all just hope-lost satellites.

Men say that they know many things,
but lo! they have only taken wings-
if they should feel the springs,
surely they’d rise high
for an ethereal life.
Love and arts and sciences
and a thousand or so appliances-
oh the wind that blows
is all anybody knows
even Henry Thoreau.
