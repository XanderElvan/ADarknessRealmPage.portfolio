Writing from the point of view of a hive mind

In this two-part prompt, we invite you to consider a new perspective:
How does it feel to be part of a hive mind- a collective whole, speaking with one voice as you traverse the vacuum of space?

To place yourself in the perspective of a hive mind, the first exercise will focus on using the first-person plural pronoun, We.
Start by considering the photo on your left (If you would like to see an enlarged version, please click here, or on the photo itself).

As you study the photograph, try to answer some of these questions:
What can we see? What can we feel- are we warm or cold? Are we excited or anxious? What can we smell? Where are we going? What do we want? Are we safe? Are we in familiar territory? Or somewhere unknown to us?

Once you feel you have a grasp on your hive mind, please click play on the soundscape to your left, and try to bring the threads of these questions together into a scene or poem, while the soundscape plays around you.

You could start by taking one or two of those questions, those that you felt revealed the most about your hive mind, then see where the soundscape takes you. You can rewind the soundscape as often as you like, or see what has formed after writing to one play through. Alternatively, if you find the soundscape too distracting, please feel free to consider the photograph again instead, this time focusing on what your hime mind can hear around them; are they alone out here?
